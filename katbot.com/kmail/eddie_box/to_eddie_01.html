Dear Eddie,

I'm sorry I've taken so long to write, but adjusting to this ridiculous situation has taken longer than I thought it would. You will not believe the circumstance in which I find myself. I'm staying with a suburban family (note, I said suburban, not urban) in a what must be the oldest and most insular suburb in all of the United States. The section of New York I am stationed in is on a long island jutting out from the East coast of the mainland. The inhabitants seem totally unaware that there is a mainland beyond the island of Manhattan. They even have their own regional accent, which is totally unlike the English I heard in the training simulations. I sometimes find it difficult to understand what they are talking about, as their inflection disrupts my translator chip.

The family I am stationed with is rather banal, but they are pleasant enough to me and have served me a great many strange foods. I don't enjoy much of what they prepare, but I have become slightly addicted to one thing: it's a salty, crispy meat they fry up in a pan. It's called "bacon" and it is wonderful! I salivate whenever I hear the adult female plop a strip on to the sizzling cooking surface. I will try to bring some back to you when I am granted restorative leave.

I unfortunately must leave off now; the family is gathering for the evening meal, for which my presence is mandatory. I will write again soon.

With much fondness,

K.
