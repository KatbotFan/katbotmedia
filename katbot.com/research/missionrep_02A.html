Mission Report #02-A


I had a rather unsettling experience today. During my mandatory Foreign Language class (I chose Italian over German and Spanish during registration) Courtney attempted to initiate a friendship with me. She smiled at me a lot through out the class; I became aware of this when I was briefly awake during conjugation of the verb "andare". Near the end of the class Mrs. Mazzuco called on Courtney, who had been waving her arm frenetically around, even though there was no question to be answered.

"Si, Courtney? Che cosa è esso?"

Courtney promptly jumped up and shot her smile around the class.

"I would like to take a minute to welcome our new foreign exchange student, Katerina, to Hicksville High. Since Katerina is the only student here who is natively fluent in a foreign language, I would like to nominate her for the recently vacated position of secretary for the Foreign Language Club. I think Katerina would be a perfect addition to our club as well as bring incentive to the other members to practice their adopted language until they, too, are fluent."

I had been trying to keep as low a profile as I could in class, so as not to draw attention to myself. That relative anonymity ended as twenty-one pairs of eyes turned in my direction.

Mrs. Mazzuco smiled broadly. "That is a splendid idea, Courtney! I think it would also be a great opportunity for Katerina to practice writing in English. I second your nomination! We will vote on it today after school during the club meeting."

I opened my mouth to decline, but I thought any protestation might draw too much attention. I've learned from observation that it's best to go along with circumstances in order to remain at the status quo.

Courtney smiled broadly at me, but it was not a smile of innocent pleasure. I could sense something darker beneath it.

Of course I won the vote. The next Foreign Language Club meeting is next month. I must mark it down in my calendar.
