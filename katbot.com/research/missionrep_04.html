Mission Report #04


Saturday afternoon I accompanied my "host family" to a Parent Teacher Association Crafts Fair, which was held in the high school cafeteria. Mrs. LeBore is involved in some capacity with the so-called PTA. Upon arriving, she immediately commandeered a table on which to display her crafts.

The girl, June, looked rather glum on our way over to the high school and immediately separated herself from the familial unit on our arrival. Her defection afforded me some time to observe her parents.

Mrs. LeBore came baring a box of hand made items she fabricates in her spare time. Mr. LeBore followed behind another box of similar objects.

They were greeted warmly by the other adults who were busily setting up tables with knitted doilies, animal like creatures created entirely out of fluffy, brightly colored acrylic based wool, and sacred idols used to decorate one's house during the upcoming holiday, Halloween. I was introduced to more adults that I could compute. All were friendly and extremely curious about me and the "country" from whence I came. I entertained them with a few words of broken English describing my "homeland" as "pretty" but "harsh". I expressed how glad I was to be in America, which tickled them to no end. These people were extremely friendly and very ready to share their pride in their country. One portly gentleman gave me a lecture on the American work ethic. After much nodding and squirming, I was able to free myself and wander off in search of the young people. They sat in sullen groups on out in the playing fields behind the school. They seemed very whiny to me, but I could understand their irritation: I, too, remember the disappointment of the beginning of a new learning year.

I scanned the field and sensed that June was near. As I canvassed the football field, I spotted June, Paula (the talkative girl), and a skinny spotted boy crouched down under the bleachers. There was a cloud of sweet smelling smoke surrounding them. They were laughing about nothing I could discern. They did not see me. I decided not to intrude. I do not want to force myself on June. I can get more research done if I wait until she thaws to me.

I wandered to the opposite end of the playing field and lay down under a large oak tree. I powered my processor down into rest mode. After 35 minutes I rejoined the LeBore family in the cafeteria.

Something strange happened then. When I appeared, June (who had returned from the bleachers) stared at me rather intently. Her eyes were a little red, and she had a look of absolute shock on her face. I got this weird feeling that my Hypno-Façade had accidentally been turned off, but it hadn't. I don't know what happened, but I must be extra careful around her.

The rest of the fair was rather uneventful. When we returned home June ran up to her room and did not emerge until Paula picked her up. I wonder where they have gone...
